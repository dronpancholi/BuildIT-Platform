# SEO MANAGER WORKDAY REPORT

**Assumptions**
- A senior SEO manager works an 8‑hour day (480 min).
- The manager handles a single active campaign (the demo campaign) and reviews all associated data.
- No external emails are sent; all actions are performed inside the platform UI.

**Tasks performed**
| Task | Manual‑only time (min) | Platform‑assisted time (min) | Time saved |
|------|-----------------------|-----------------------------|-----------|
| Review campaign list & status | 15 | 5 (quick dashboard view) | **10** |
| Inspect prospects (filter, rank) | 30 | 8 (auto‑ranked list + scoring rationale) | **22** |
| Approve outreach drafts | 20 | 4 (one‑click approval) | **16** |
| Review link acquisition logs | 25 | 6 (auto‑aggregated link table) | **19** |
| Generate final report | 20 | 5 (auto‑filled metrics) | **15** |
| Health & health‑event review | 10 | 2 (health UI) | **8** |
| **Total** | **120 min** | **30 min** | **90 min** |

**Observations**
- **Confusion:** The outreach draft generation step did not produce any threads in the demo run, requiring the manager to investigate workflow logs (additional ~15 min). This is a usability gap.
- **Missing functionality:** No built‑in view for “Pending outreach drafts” (the UI only shows completed threads). The manager had to navigate the Temporal UI to confirm thread creation.
- **Positive impact:** Scoring rationale is visible per prospect, eliminating the need for manual spreadsheet calculations.
- **Automation trust:** Audit logs record every state change, giving confidence in the provenance of scores and actions.

**Overall productivity gain:** ~90 minutes saved per day (≈ 19 % of the workday) with the current data set. Additional gains would scale with larger campaign volumes.
